Innovate.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can participate in NadiCare? +
Any student can participate! Teams can be from different colleges and different disciplines. There's no restriction on your branch or year ‚Äî all backgrounds are welcome as long as you're passionate about healthcare technology.
What is the team size? +
Teams must have a minimum of 2 and a maximum of 3 members. Solo participation is not allowed. You can team up with students from your own college or from different colleges.
How do I get the discounted registration fee? +
If even one member of your team holds an IEEE, SPS, EMBS, IEI, or ISTE membership, your entire team qualifies for the discounted rate of ‚Çπ499. The IEEE Member rate of ‚Çπ599 applies if any member holds a general IEEE membership.
When will the problem statements be released? +
Problem statements will be released on 1st March 2026. You'll have time to research and brainstorm before the hackathon day on 14th March 2026.
What should we bring on the hackathon day? +
Bring your laptops, chargers, and any hardware components your solution requires. Participants are responsible for arranging their own hardware. Make sure to also carry your college ID card.
What will we be judged on? +
Teams must demonstrate a working prototype, simulation, or validated algorithm. You'll present your solution on the hackathon day. Industry mentors will guide you throughout the process leading up to the final demonstration.
Will there be mentorship during the hackathon? +
Yes! One of the highlights of NadiCare is access to industry mentors throughout the entire process ‚Äî not just on the day. Partners like GTM4Health, Nexstem, S-VYASA and others will be involved in guiding teams.
How do I register? +
Fill out the registration form at the link below before 28th February 2026. Registration is per team, not per individual. Make sure all team member details are filled in correctly.
